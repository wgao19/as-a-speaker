depth of learning

Then, both of us were more of a fan for going deeper into a smaller set of knowledge, rather than a shallow overview of a lot of things. We want our girls to really get hold of a skill at the end of the thirty days.
So we kind of decided that we'll just do CSS for thirty days.
And that our first challenge was going to draw a heart with CSS.

And can anybody claim that there is nothing to learn even when you are working on something quite "easy"? Later on we were lucky to have Huijing to join us as a mentor, who shared with us what she learned working on the heart in a blog post.

Then, we kind of just started the process of writing blog posts about things we learned about css. And started merging each others' posts to our static site.

Speaking of this, I'd like to mention that some of our girls have very little programming background, and joined the challenge just because they want to check out something cool. One girl, who does food critique part time, is picking up markdown and OS contribution on the side.

I think a lot of girl, self-taught, front end developers are quite amazing, if you consider what kind of training she has. By figuring a lot of things out by herself, there's a long way to go even before a first full time job. There are many, many such stories out there, in here.

Maybe next time you can hear some stories from such developers around you, and maybe next next time when you see her trying to make a contribution on her merge request, maybe you can spend more time to discuss and guide her to the right direction.

CSS specs
We've given CSS specs a high recognition throughout the challenges. In fact, on alternate days, we are not assigning new challenges, instead we want our participants to devote time into specs reading.
What is CSS Specifications?
Depending on who I'm talking to, choose from:
  • no need to explain at all
  • PRD written by CSS experts that browser implementors are recommended to follow
