As a matter of fact, not only 30 days of challenge, someone put #30daysofcode, #100daysofcode, _and_ #301DaysOfCode on one single tweet.

This guys is on a mission towards a 301 days coding challenge covering basically everything we use and not use. We should definitely hire him after he's done!!!

I guess by the time that was both a behavior I did not fully understand, and something I cannot reject lol.
So we went ahead explore the possibility. Do we want to do a web development crash course? Do we want to include JavaScript? And Ann kind of just has to bear with me as a React developer lol.

So based on who we are

girl devs (or not)

self-taught

we want to create things good looking

we kind of decided on the direction to go

girls love good looking things

Actually, everybody does. But girls are more external about this kind of points I believe. We're quite comfortable saying or admitting that we do things for appearance reasons.

depth of learning

Then, both of us were more of a fan for going deeper into a smaller set of knowledge, rather than a shallow overview of a lot of things. We want our girls to really get hold of a skill at the end of the thirty days.
So we kind of decided that we'll just do CSS for thirty days.
And that our first challenge was going to draw a heart with CSS.

See the Pen Day 1 - Confession #30DaysOfCSSGirls by Jia Qian Koh (@swifty_star4) on CodePen.

We did not give very detailed step-by-step requirement for what we wanted our girls to create. With the space for creativity, you never know.
The Anatomy of a Pure CSS Heart ❤
And can anybody claim that there is nothing to learn even when you are working on something quite "easy"? Later on we were lucky to have Huijing to join us as a mentor, who shared with us what she learned working on the heart in a blog post.

Then, we kind of just started the process of writing blog posts about things we learned about css. And started merging each others' posts to our static site.

Speaking of this, I'd like to mention that some of our girls have very little programming background, and joined the challenge just because they want to check out something cool. One girl, who does food critique part time, is picking up markdown and OS contribution on the side.

I think a lot of girl, self-taught, front end developers are quite amazing, if you consider what kind of systematic training she has. By figuring a lot of things out by herself, there's a long way to go even before a first full time job. There are many, many such stories out there, in here.

Maybe next time you can hear some stories from such developers around you, and maybe next next time when you see her trying to make a contribution on her merge request you'd pay more time to discuss and guide her to the right direction.

And we have a static site built with Gatsby which shares the same typographic theme as my slides today. We will keep this site here. So you may pick up come of the challenges any time in the future.
