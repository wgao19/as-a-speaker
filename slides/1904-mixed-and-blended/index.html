section 10 of the specs

Cr = (1 - ab) x Cs + ab x B(Cb, Cs)

  • Cr, Cs, Cb - resulting color, source color, backdrop color
  • ab - alpha of backdrop
  • blending modes specify B(Cb, Cs)

The result of the mixing function is modulated by the backdrop alpha. A fully opaque backdrop allows the mixing function to be fully realized. A transparent backdrop will cause the final result to be a weighted average between the source color and mixed color with the weight controlled by the backdrop alpha.

To understand the properties of ‚Äúmultiply‚Äù, we need to understand the RGB color model.

The RGB color model is additive in the sense that the three light beams are added together.

When all three beams are at the strongest intensity, we get white. When the beams are less intense but are equal in their intensity, we get a point on the greyscale. When they have different intensity, we get a colorized hue.

The RGB color model represents choices of colors in the color cube. It‚Äôs a unit cube. This means that red, blue, and green sit on the ‚Äú1‚Äù ends of the axis. The ‚Äú1‚Äù means that color beam at its highest intensity, and ‚Äú0‚Äù means no light at all. So RGB color model is based on Cartesian coordinate system.

"multiply"

The HEX color codes we use then divides each axis to 256 partitions. So each point represents a fraction of that 1. So when you ‚Äúmultiply‚Äù two colors under the RGB model, you are multiplying the fractions of 1. What it actually means is how the two color beams weaken each other. And since each one of the three color beams are either left unchanged (if multiplied with 1) or weakened, the result is at least as dark as either of the two colors. It follows that multiplying any color with black gives you black, like you turn off the lights. And multiplying any color white white gives you the original color, like no weakening at all.

That's like the most subtle definition of "multiply" I've ever seen
